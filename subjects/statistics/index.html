üéØ Subject Overview

Statistics is the science of learning from data, providing the mathematical foundation for making informed decisions under uncertainty. This subject covers comprehensive statistical knowledge from basic probability theory to advanced modeling techniques used in modern data science and research.

From understanding sampling distributions to building complex regression models, this journey will develop your statistical reasoning and practical skills. We'll explore both frequentist and Bayesian approaches, with emphasis on real-world applications and computational implementation.

üéì Learning Goals

By the end of this 40-week journey, you will master:

üé≤ Probability & Foundations

  • Probability Theory: Sample spaces, conditional probability, Bayes' theorem, and probability distributions
  • Random Variables: Discrete and continuous distributions, expectation, variance, and moment generating functions
  • Limit Theorems: Law of large numbers, central limit theorem, and convergence concepts
  • Multivariate Distributions: Joint, marginal, conditional distributions, covariance, and independence

üìà Statistical Inference

  • Estimation Theory: Point estimation, maximum likelihood, method of moments, bias and efficiency
  • Confidence Intervals: Construction methods, interpretation, and coverage properties
  • Hypothesis Testing: Test construction, power analysis, p-values, and multiple testing corrections
  • Nonparametric Methods: Rank tests, bootstrap methods, and distribution-free procedures

üî¨ Regression & Modeling

  • Linear Models: Simple and multiple regression, ANOVA, model diagnostics, and assumptions
  • Generalized Linear Models: Logistic regression, Poisson regression, and exponential families
  • Mixed Effects Models: Random effects, hierarchical models, and clustered data analysis
  • Model Selection: Information criteria, cross-validation, regularization, and variable selection

üß† Bayesian Statistics

  • Bayesian Fundamentals: Prior distributions, posterior inference, and credible intervals
  • Computational Methods: MCMC, Gibbs sampling, Hamiltonian Monte Carlo
  • Hierarchical Models: Bayesian multilevel modeling and shrinkage estimation
  • Model Comparison: Bayes factors, information criteria, and posterior predictive checks

üîç Advanced Topics

  • Causal Inference: Potential outcomes, propensity scores, instrumental variables, DAGs
  • Survival Analysis: Time-to-event data, Kaplan-Meier estimation, Cox regression
  • Time Series: ARIMA models, state space models, and forecasting techniques
  • Experimental Design: Randomization, blocking, factorial designs, and optimal design theory
Computational Focus: Every topic includes implementation in R and Python, with emphasis on reproducible analysis workflows. We'll use real datasets, explore data visualization techniques, and learn to communicate statistical findings effectively. Both theoretical understanding and practical application are emphasized throughout.
