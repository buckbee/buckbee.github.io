üí° Key Concepts

Noise Sources in Transistors

There are three primary noise sources in MOS transistors that affect LNA performance:

  • Thermal noise: Channel resistance creates thermal noise with power spectral density of 4kTR
  • Flicker (1/f) noise: Dominant at low frequencies, inversely proportional to gate area
  • Induced gate noise: Capacitive coupling of channel noise to the gate terminal

Noise Matching vs. Power Matching

This is the fundamental challenge in LNA design:

  • Power matching: Requires source impedance ZS = Zin* for maximum power transfer
  • Noise matching: Requires ZS = Zopt for minimum noise figure
  • Unfortunately, Zopt ‚â† Zin* in general!
Design Trade-off: You must choose between optimum noise or optimum gain. Practical LNAs use matching networks or inductive degeneration to achieve a compromise.

Inductive Source Degeneration

The clever solution: adding an inductor LS in series with the source creates a real component in the input impedance that can be tuned to match 50Œ© while simultaneously achieving near-optimal noise performance.

üìù Key Insights & Notes

What I Learned

  1. Larger transistors ‚â† always better: While increasing W reduces gate resistance noise, it also increases capacitance and induced gate noise. There's an optimal width.
  2. Bias current matters: Higher gm (from higher ID) improves noise, but at the cost of power consumption. Real designs must balance both.
  3. Frequency dependency: At lower frequencies, flicker noise dominates. At high frequencies (>1GHz), thermal and induced gate noise are primary concerns.
  4. Technology scaling impact: Modern short-channel devices have higher Œ≥ factors (approaching 2-3 vs. 2/3 for long-channel), making noise optimization more challenging.

Practical Design Guidelines

  • For narrowband applications: Use inductive degeneration for simultaneous noise and power matching
  • For wideband applications: Consider resistive feedback or noise cancellation techniques
  • Always check device operating point: deep triode or cutoff regions have terrible noise performance
  • Don't forget layout: minimize gate resistance by proper finger layout and multiple gate contacts
