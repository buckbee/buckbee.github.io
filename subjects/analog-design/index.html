üéØ Subject Overview

Analog design is the art and science of creating circuits that process continuous signals in the real world. This subject covers advanced topics in analog integrated circuit design, focusing on CMOS and bipolar technologies used in modern electronic systems.

From precision operational amplifiers to gigahertz RF circuits, analog design requires deep understanding of device physics, circuit analysis, and practical design trade-offs. This 40-week journey will take you from fundamental building blocks to complete system-level designs.

üéì Learning Goals

By the end of this 40-week journey, you will master:

üî¨ Core Analog Fundamentals

  • Device Physics: Deep understanding of MOS and bipolar transistor behavior, parasitic effects, and process variations
  • Small-Signal Analysis: Advanced techniques for analyzing complex multi-stage amplifiers and feedback systems
  • Noise Analysis: Comprehensive noise modeling, optimization techniques, and measurement methods
  • Frequency Response: Pole-zero analysis, compensation techniques, and stability analysis

‚ö° Advanced Circuit Techniques

  • Operational Amplifiers: Design of high-performance op-amps with rail-to-rail operation and low offset
  • Comparators: High-speed voltage comparators with hysteresis and precision timing
  • Voltage References: Bandgap references, temperature compensation, and precision voltage sources
  • Current Sources: Precision current mirrors, current references, and bias circuits

üîÑ Data Conversion Systems

  • ADC Architectures: SAR, Delta-Sigma, pipeline, and flash converters with performance trade-offs
  • DAC Design: Current steering, R-2R, and sigma-delta DACs for different applications
  • Sample-and-Hold: High-speed sampling circuits with low distortion and fast settling
  • Digital Calibration: Background calibration techniques for improving converter performance

üì° RF & High-Speed Design

  • Low-Noise Amplifiers: RF LNA design with simultaneous noise and input matching
  • Mixers & Oscillators: Gilbert cell mixers, VCOs, and phase-locked loops
  • Power Amplifiers: Class A/B/D amplifiers with efficiency optimization
  • High-Speed Interfaces: CML logic, differential signaling, and equalization techniques

üîã Power Management

  • Linear Regulators: LDO design with fast transient response and low dropout
  • Switching Regulators: Buck, boost, and buck-boost converter design
  • Power Factor Correction: Active PFC circuits for AC-DC conversion
  • Battery Management: Charging circuits, protection, and fuel gauging
Design Philosophy: Throughout this journey, we'll emphasize practical design considerations including process variations, temperature effects, packaging constraints, and cost optimization. Every topic includes both theoretical analysis and hands-on simulation work.
